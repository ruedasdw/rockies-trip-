Weather & What to Pack

Mid-July in the Rockies 🌤️

68–77°F
Daytime highs
45–54°F
Morning / evening
Colder
At lakes & gondola

Warm sunny afternoons, but it cools off fast in the mornings, evenings, and up at altitude (lakeshores and the gondola run chilly even in summer). Quick afternoon showers pop up, and the mountain sun is strong. The golden rule: dress in layers — start in a t-shirt, add a fleece and a rain shell as you climb.

🧥 Clothing

  • Base layers: t-shirts + a few quick-dry / moisture-wicking tops (better than cotton)
  • Mid layer: a fleece or packable down/puffer for each person — the key warmth layer
  • Rain shell: a light waterproof jacket each (afternoon showers are common)
  • Bottoms: mostly shorts, plus lightweight long pants for cool evenings & trails
  • Warm extras: a beanie & light gloves for the kids on early/high outings
  • Swimsuits: for hot springs, lakeside, and the pool at every hotel
  • Sleepwear & a warm outfit for chilly mountain mornings

👟 Footwear & Sun

  • Closed-toe walking / light hiking shoes for everyone (trails & uneven ground)
  • Sandals or slip-ons for the pool, hot springs & relaxing after hikes
  • Comfortable everyday sneakers for town & the long drive days
  • Warm socks (a couple of wool-ish pairs help on cool mornings)
  • Sunscreen & sunglasses — UV is strong at elevation
  • Sun hats / caps for all six of us
  • Reusable water bottles & a small daypack for outings
  • Bear spray for trails — buy it locally in Canmore/Banff (it can't go on the plane)

👶 For the Little Ones

  • Child carrier / backpack for canyon walks & trails where strollers don't fit
  • Car seats for the 3- & 5-year-old (required in Alberta) — we're bringing our own
  • Layers that are easy on/off — kids feel the temperature swings most
  • Snacks, wipes & entertainment for the longer drive days (Icefields Parkway & Jasper→Calgary)
  • Stay bear-aware: keep the kids close on trails, make noise, and never leave food out at viewpoints
